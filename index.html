About

From kernel to product.

A decade-plus deep in Android. Now shipping it as a product.

I'm Aryan Sinha, a Technical Product Manager and AOSP engineer based in India. I currently work at Volla Systeme GmbH, where I sit between engineering and product, shaping how Volla's privacy-first Android devices come together end to end.

I'm also the founder and lead developer of CipherOS, an open-source, privacy-first AOSP-based operating system. We ship a de-Googled, zero-telemetry experience with our own design language, Seamless Aqua, and a growing suite of first-party Cipher apps.

My background runs from kernels and BSPs to product roadmaps and release management. Most days look like reading a HAL diff in the morning, writing a product brief over lunch, and triaging a build break in the evening.

What I work on

Where I spend my time.

01

Product & Roadmap

Translating engineering reality into product direction. Release planning, scope cuts, OEM conversations, and writing the briefs that get the right thing shipped.

02

Android Framework

System UI, SystemServer, telephony, sensors. The layer where AOSP becomes something users actually notice, and where most of my code lives.

03

BSP & HAL

Bringing up MediaTek silicon on clean AOSP. Reverse-engineering vendor blobs, taming HALs, and making things that were never meant to work upstream, work upstream.

04

Kernel

Patches, backports, and the occasional revert. Keeping older-kernel devices alive on modern Android, because not everyone gets to ship a fresh GKI tree.

05

Privacy Engineering

Designing for zero telemetry as a default, not a setting. Working out how on-device AI, app compatibility, and de-Googling coexist without the user having to think about it.

06

Open Source Stewardship

Running a community-built OS. Reviews, governance, release management, and making sure the people who show up to contribute have a good time doing it.
